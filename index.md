# Causal reasoning with DAGs — CBCD Causal Inference Workshop (28th April 2026)

This repository hosts the materials for the tutorial workshop on DAGs (directed acyclic graphs) at the CBCD Causal Inference Workshop.

**Abstract**: This talk will introduce key technical aspects of directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) as a tool for representing causal models. We will focus on the testable implications of DAGs and how they can be used to reason about confounding, conditioning, and covariate adjustment. I will also demonstrate how software tools such as dagitty (available both as an R package and online at [dagitty.net](https://dagitty.net/)) can be used to check graphical implications and identify appropriate adjustment sets. The session will conclude with a "draw your DAG" activity in which participants sketch a causal diagram for their own research questions.
